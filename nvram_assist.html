NorthTek to NVRAM creator


This configurator takes the NorthTek script and modifies it to be placed in NVRAM on the AHRS-8, GEDC-6E, and the DC-4E

Key things to note here are:
* 60 max characters per line
* There is a userOpen and userClose
* The address for user space is 0x10000
* "put" is actually a NorthTek 'word' or function that enables us to place more characters per line

The AHRS-8, GEDC-6E, DC-4E, and the AHRS-M2 all have an seperate EEPROM chip that holds the contents of NorthTek scripts to be sent to the processor upon power-up. This is the space we call NVRAM. This NVRAM space only holds the ASCII version of the script. The script will be sent tot he FORTH stack upon pwoer-up and will compiled at run-time. The larger the script, the longer it will take to wake up the part. All of the current user scripts are small enough that the time to first good reading will not be affected by a large script.
